**Age:** 35
**Occupation:** Operations Manager
**Location:** Accra, Ghana
**Family Status:** Married, 2 children

---

## Background

Sarah manages a team of 15 people handling COD orders for an e-commerce company. She's been in operations for 8 years and is responsible for ensuring timely deliveries, customer satisfaction, and team performance. She juggles multiple systems and relies heavily on reports to make decisions.

---

## Goals & Motivations

**Primary Goal:**
Maintain >95% delivery success rate while keeping costs under control

**Secondary Goals:**
- Quickly identify and resolve delivery issues
- Track team performance and provide coaching
- Generate accurate reports for management

**Motivations:**
- Professional growth and promotion opportunities
- Team success and recognition
- Efficient processes that save time
- Data-driven decision making

---

## Pain Points & Frustrations

**Major Pain Points:**
1. Manual data entry wastes hours each week
2. Delayed or inaccurate reports make it hard to respond to issues quickly
3. Communication gaps between delivery agents and office staff

**Daily Frustrations:**
- Switching between 4 different systems to get a complete picture
- Chasing down delivery agents for status updates
- Customers calling for updates that should be automated
- Spreadsheet errors from manual data compilation

---

## Behaviors & Habits

**Work Patterns:**
- Checks dashboard first thing in morning
- Responds to urgent issues immediately
- Reviews reports weekly for trends
- Holds daily standup with team at 9 AM

**Technology Usage:**
- Devices: Desktop at office, smartphone for field updates
- Comfort level: High - comfortable with complex software
- Preferred tools: Slack, Google Sheets, WhatsApp for team communication

**Communication Preferences:**
- Slack for team updates
- Email for official communications
- Phone calls for urgent issues only
- Weekly in-person team meetings

---

## Tech Proficiency

[✓] High - Power user, expects advanced features

---

## Quote

> "I don't have time to log into 4 different systems just to answer a simple question about an order. Give me one dashboard that shows me everything."
>
> — Sarah Chen

---

## Design Implications

**What this means for our design:**

✅ **Do:**
- Provide comprehensive dashboard with real-time updates
- Enable bulk actions for common tasks
- Include advanced filters and search
- Support keyboard shortcuts for efficiency
- Provide exportable reports in multiple formats

❌ **Don't:**
- Hide important metrics in submenus
- Require page refreshes for status updates
- Make simple tasks require multiple clicks
- Force single-item operations when bulk is needed
- Limit filtering or sorting capabilities
